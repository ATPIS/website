About Us

SSCC Kfarhbab's First Student Club

ATPIS is a student-based and student-led organization comprising students from the secondary classes of Collège des Saints Cœurs Kfarhbab. Its mission is to support projects initiated by one or more club members in scientific, computer, technical, and related subjects, operating on a voluntary basis.

Groups

ATPIS is structured into multiple distinct groups, each specializing in key scientific areas

The Chemistry Group

Step into the captivating world of molecules and reactions with our chemistry group, where bubbling concoctions, colourful reactions, and mind-bending experiments await

The Math & Physics Group

Join the Math and Physics group for an adventure into the world of numbers and natural laws, where we explore the beauty of mathematical patterns and unlock the secrets of the fundamental forces shaping our universe

The Biology & Ecology Group

Dive into the fascinating realms of biology and ecology with our group, where the wonders of ecosystems and ecological interactions unfold

The Computer Science, Electronics & Robotics Group

The Computer Science, Electronics & Robotics Group dives into the realm of coding, circuitry, and automation, igniting creativity and innovation in software development and robotics projects

ATPIS & ATPIS Jr

ATPIS is open to students ranging from 13 to 18, bringing together a diverse community passionate about technology and science.

The Main Branch

Members of ATPIS's main branch have the opportunity to engage in world-class competitions, take on leadership roles within the club, and develop invaluable skills essential for personal and academic growth.

"From small beginnings comes great success"

In an effort to suit students of all ages, we are lanching a new branch of the club named ATPIS "Junior" for all students between the ages of 13 and 15.

  • Design age-appropriate activities and projects that align with the interests and capabilities of middle school students, fostering their curiosity and engagement in STEM fields.
  • Provide mentorship programs where older students or volunteers can guide and support middle school members, helping them develop skills, confidence, and a passion for learning in a supportive environment.
  • Encourage middle school students to explore different aspects of science and technology through competitions and interactive sessions, promoting critical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving skills.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common queries and gain valuable insights into our club and activities.

1. What is the main goal of the student club?

Our main goal is to promote interest in sciences through practical activities, research projects, scientific competitions, and conferences with experts.

2. What are the benefits of joining the club?

The club provides a vibrant social environment where members interact, collaborate, and form lasting friendships with peers, fostering a supportive community beyond the classroom. Active participation in club activities, such as competitions, workshops, and projects, enriches a student's resume, showcasing their dedication to scientific exploration, teamwork skills, and leadership potential, which are highly valued by academic institutions.

3. How does ATPIS plan to support the sustainability of its activities for future years?

We rely on active participation from its current and future members, as well as developing partnerships with experts, sponsors, and institutions to ensure ongoing resources and support. Additionally, the creation of the ATPIS Jr. branch aims to ensure the club's renewal and growth over the years.

4. What opportunities are available to the club members in terms of practical activities and scientific competitions?

ATPIS club members have access to practical workshops where they can conduct scientific experiments, research projects to explore topics of interest, and they have the opportunity to participate in local, national, and international scientific competitions to apply their scientific knowledge and skills.

5. How does the club encourage collaboration and exchange of ideas among its members?

We try to foster collaboration and information exchange among members of different grades by organizing mixed-grade teams for research projects, competitions, and workshops. This approach encourages peer learning, cross-grade mentorship, and the sharing of diverse perspectives and experiences, creating a dynamic and inclusive environment that enhances overall learning.
